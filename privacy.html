Privacy Policy

Prototype draft. Last updated June 2, 2026.

STRIKEPULSE is designed around privacy-first trading workflow support. The current prototype stores preferences, watchlists, alerts, journal entries, notifications, and paper-trading history locally in the browser.

The prototype does not require brokerage credentials, payment card details, real account balances, government IDs, or sensitive personal information.

Journal notes are redacted for common email, phone, ID, and card-like patterns before saving locally. Users should still avoid entering sensitive personal information.

AI Privacy Mode is on by default. AI review payloads should include trade context, not identity, payment data, brokerage credentials, or private account details.

Backend integrations may call market-data, AI, billing, authentication, or notification providers in future versions. API keys and service credentials must remain server-side and must not be exposed in browser-delivered files.

When cloud sync is added, STRIKEPULSE should clearly identify what data is stored, why it is stored, how users can export it, and how users can delete it.

This privacy policy is a product-readiness draft and should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before commercial launch.
